### 2. `reference` must appear as the shared root word in both the folder and the single file

This is the single most important structural principle identified. When both artifacts share the same root and differ only in their modifier, an AI instantly infers: *same content, different packaging*. When the root word differs, the AI assumes they are different things entirely.

**Example of the principle working:**

The shared `-reference` suffix establishes peer authority. The differing prefixes communicate only the packaging difference.

**Example of the principle failing:**

An AI sees two unrelated concepts and must read files to understand the relationship.

- They are a matched set

Multiple sources converge on this: dropping the `{section}-` prefix from files *inside* the section folder is a meaningful usability improvement. Generic folder names like `pages/`, `topics/`, `sections/`, or `docs/` fail because they give no hint of their peer relationship to the single combined file. The folder name needs a modifier that explicitly contrasts with the single file's modifier.
